## 1. Research Summary

### 1.1 What the Best Finance-Tech Bridge Professionals Do

Research into personal branding at the intersection of finance and technology reveals several patterns among effective practitioners:

- **They lead with the intersection, not either discipline alone.** The unique value is not "I know finance" or "I know AI" -- it is "I translate between these two worlds." This framing positions the individual as a rare connector rather than a generalist.
- **They use narrative coherence to defeat the "generalist" stigma.** Interdisciplinary professionals often face the challenge of being perceived as unfocused. The antidote is a story where every transition was an intentional step toward a unified mission (source: Aalto IDBM alumni case study).
- **Trust is paramount when money is involved.** Fintech branding research emphasizes that credibility signals (institutions, quantified achievements, testimonials) must be woven into the narrative, not relegated to a separate section.

### 1.2 Storytelling Techniques That Work

The CARS framework (Challenge, Action, Result, Significance) is the gold standard for structuring career bullet points. For the overarching narrative, the three-act structure (Setup, Tension/Turning Point, Resolution/Vision) maps naturally onto Wing's journey.

Key finding: authenticity outperforms polish. Audiences connect with honest motivation and real struggle, not a curated highlight reel. The research strongly recommends a conversational tone with specific anecdotes rather than abstract claims.

### 1.3 Scroll-Driven Storytelling (Scrollytelling)

Scrollytelling -- content that unfolds dynamically as the user scrolls -- achieves up to 400% higher engagement than static pages (per data from NYT, National Geographic, and The Pudding). Core principles:

- Use vertical scrolling as the primary narrative driver (familiar, accessible)
- Trigger animations/transitions at narrative beats, not arbitrarily
- Follow the inverted-U of complexity: enough detail to engage, not enough to overwhelm
- Mobile-first: reduce animation complexity for smaller devices
- Accessibility: all scroll-triggered content must be accessible without JavaScript

## 2. Narrative Architecture

### 2.1 The Core Story (Three Acts)

Wing's story is fundamentally about **crossing borders** -- geographic, disciplinary, and cultural -- in pursuit of a singular mission: making finance more intelligent through technology.

**Act I -- The Crossing (Setup)**
A teenager in Hong Kong takes the leap to become a US exchange student in North Carolina. This is the origin story: the willingness to leave the familiar, to operate in ambiguity, to bridge two worlds. Community college in Washington state, 4.0 GPA -- not the glamorous path, but the proving ground. Then UW, double major in Finance and Information Systems. The bridge begins to take shape.

**Act II -- The Building (Tension & Growth)**
Costco: the first professional chapter. Wing automates financial processes, saving hundreds of hours. The pattern emerges -- using technology to make finance work better. Then Carta, where the pattern deepens: redesigning PE workflows and building LLM tools for private equity analysis. The career is not a pivot; it is an escalation. Each role asks the same question with higher stakes: "What happens when you give a finance mind engineering tools?"

**Act III -- The Synthesis (Resolution & Vision)**
The return to Hong Kong. MSc AI at HKUST. The geographic circle closes: HK to US and back. But now the disciplinary circle closes too: researching LLM-driven macro asset allocation. The exchange student who left Hong Kong to learn returns with the tools to build the future of finance. And along the way: 5x guest speaker, CFO of a blockchain society, mentor to others making their own crossings.

### 2.2 The Hook (Homepage Opening)

The homepage must answer three questions within 5 seconds:
1. Who is this person?
2. What do they do that is different?
3. Why should I care?

**Recommended hook strategy: The Paradox Opening.**

Rather than a generic "Hi, I'm Wing" or a list of credentials, open with the inherent tension that defines Wing's value:

> "Finance speaks one language. AI speaks another. I build the bridge."

This single line establishes the positioning, creates intrigue, and invites the visitor to scroll further to understand how.

### 2.3 Positioning Statement (Unique Value Proposition)

After extensive analysis, the recommended positioning:

**Core positioning:**
> "Finance-trained, AI-fluent. I sit at the intersection where financial intuition meets machine intelligence -- building tools, workflows, and research that make capital allocation smarter."

**Extended positioning (for About section):**
> "I have spent my career crossing borders -- from Hong Kong to the US and back, from spreadsheets to LLMs, from executing financial analysis to automating it. At every step, the question has been the same: how do we make finance more intelligent? Today, as an MSc AI candidate at HKUST, I research how large language models can drive macro asset allocation. Previously, I built LLM tools at Carta and automated financial systems at Costco. I speak English, Cantonese, and Mandarin. I bridge cultures the same way I bridge disciplines: by listening carefully to both sides."

### 2.4 The Journey as Intentional, Not Accidental

The biggest risk for interdisciplinary professionals is the perception of randomness. Wing's narrative must present the finance-to-AI transition not as a pivot but as a **deepening**:

| Career Stage | Narrative Frame | Underlying Thread |
|---|---|---|
| UW Double Major (Finance + IS) | "I studied both because even then I knew finance needed better tools." | Technology amplifies financial thinking |
| Costco Financial Analyst | "I saw hundreds of manual hours and asked: what if a machine did this?" | Automation as the natural question |
| Carta PE Analyst | "I redesigned workflows and built LLM tools -- the question escalated from 'automate this task' to 'can AI think about markets?'" | From task automation to intelligent systems |
| HKUST MSc AI | "Now I am researching the answer." | Research as the culmination |

Each step is the same impulse at a higher level of abstraction. The narrative must make this explicit.

### 2.5 Weaving In Speaking, Mentorship, and Leadership

These should NOT be a separate "Activities" section that feels like resume padding. Instead, they should be integrated as evidence of the bridge-builder identity:

- **5x guest speaker**: "I do not just build bridges between finance and AI -- I help others see why the bridge matters." Frame speaking as thought leadership and translation work.
- **Blockchain society CFO**: Frame as walking the talk -- leading a finance function within a tech-forward organization.
- **Mentorship**: Frame as the most human form of bridge-building. The exchange student who was once mentored now mentors others making their own crossings. This closes the narrative loop.

### 2.6 Tone and Voice

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident but not arrogant** | States achievements through impact, not self-praise | "Saved hundreds of hours" not "I am exceptional at automation" |
| **Conversational but precise** | Reads like a smart friend explaining their work | No jargon walls, but no dumbing down either |
| **Warm but professional** | Approachable -- reflects the person who mentors and speaks publicly | Not corporate-stiff, not casually informal |
| **Forward-looking** | Each past achievement points to a future direction | "This led me to ask..." rather than "I accomplished..." |
| **Cross-cultural awareness** | Reflects someone who operates in multiple languages and cultures | Subtle, not performative -- let the HK/US/HK journey do the work |

**Voice principle**: Write as if speaking to a smart peer at a conference coffee break. Not a keynote speech. Not a cover letter. A genuine conversation with someone who is curious about what you do.

## 3. Content Structure & Page Architecture

### 3.1 Hero Section

**Layout**: Full-viewport hero with minimal elements.

**Content hierarchy** (in order of visual priority):
1. **Headline**: "Finance speaks one language. AI speaks another. I build the bridge."
2. **Subheadline**: "Wing Chun Leung -- MSc AI at HKUST | Former PE Analyst at Carta | Building intelligent financial systems"
3. **Scroll indicator**: A subtle downward animation inviting exploration
4. **Optional**: A single, high-quality professional photo (not a headshot grid -- one image that conveys approachability and intelligence)

**HCI justification**:
- Visceral level: Clean, high-contrast design creates immediate trust and sophistication
- Behavioral level: Single clear action (scroll) reduces decision paralysis
- H3 (Minimalism): No competing elements. One message, one action.
- H7 (Visibility): Scroll indicator provides system status ("there is more below")

### 3.2 Narrative Scroll Section ("The Journey")

This is the signature section. As the user scrolls, Wing's story unfolds in a scroll-driven timeline. The journey is presented as a geographic and disciplinary arc:

**Scroll Sequence**:

```
[Scroll Position 1] -- Origin
  Hong Kong --> "Where I learned to see the world from the East"
  Visual: Stylized HK skyline or abstract geographic element

[Scroll Position 2] -- The Leap
  North Carolina (Exchange Student) --> "Where I learned to see it from the West"
  Visual: Transition animation from East to West

[Scroll Position 3] -- The Proving Ground
  Washington State (Community College, 4.0) --> "Where I proved that path matters less than drive"
  Visual: Academic/growth imagery

[Scroll Position 4] -- The Foundation
  University of Washington (Finance + IS) --> "Where the bridge began"
  Visual: Two disciplines converging

[Scroll Position 5] -- The Builder
  Costco (Financial Systems Analyst) --> "Where I asked: what if machines did the repetitive work?"
  Carta (PE Analyst) --> "Where I answered: they can -- and they can do more"
  Visual: Automation/tool-building imagery

[Scroll Position 6] -- The Return
  HKUST (MSc AI) --> "Where it all comes together"
  Visual: The circle closes -- HK skyline again, but now with AI/tech overlay

[Scroll Position 7] -- The Vision
  "LLM-driven macro allocation" --> "Where finance is going"
  Visual: Forward-looking, abstract
```

**HCI justification**:
- Gestalt Continuation: The scroll path creates a perceived line connecting all career stages
- Cognitive Load Theory: Each scroll position presents ONE idea (reduces extraneous load)
- Mental Model: The geographic arc (HK -> US -> HK) provides a spatial mental model that makes the career easy to remember
- H6 (Recognition over Recall): The visual timeline means visitors never need to remember where they are in the story
- H7 (Visibility): Scroll progress indicator shows position in the narrative

### 3.3 About Section

**Structure**: Two-column layout on desktop, stacked on mobile.

**Left column**: Professional photo (different from hero) or an abstract visual element.

**Right column**: The extended positioning statement (Section 2.3), followed by three short paragraphs:

1. **The Professional** (2-3 sentences): Current work at HKUST, research focus, what excites Wing about LLM + finance.
2. **The Bridge-Builder** (2-3 sentences): Speaking, mentorship, blockchain society. Frame as translation and service.
3. **The Human** (2-3 sentences): Christian faith as a grounding force. Trilingual. The person behind the professional.

**HCI justification**:
- Gestalt Proximity: Grouping related information into three clear clusters
- H3 (Minimalism): Three paragraphs, not a wall of text
- Norman's Reflective Level: This section is where visitors form their impression of Wing as a person -- it must resonate emotionally, not just informationally

### 3.4 Experience Section

**Recommended format: Narrative cards, not a bare timeline.**

A pure timeline (date | company | title) is a resume, not a story. Instead, use cards that each tell a micro-story:

```
[Card: Carta]
  Role: PE Analyst
  The Question: "Can LLMs transform private equity analysis?"
  The Answer: Redesigned deal workflows. Built LLM-powered research tools.
  The Impact: [Quantified where possible]

[Card: Costco]
  Role: Financial Systems Analyst
  The Question: "What if we automated the manual work?"
  The Answer: Built automated financial processes.
  The Impact: Saved hundreds of hours of manual work.
```

Each card follows the same structure (Question -> Answer -> Impact), creating internal consistency (H1) and making the cards scannable (H6).

**Ordering**: Reverse chronological (most recent first), but with the "question" framing showing how each role built on the previous one.

**HCI justification**:
- H1 (Consistency): Identical card structure across all experiences
- H6 (Recognition): The Q/A/Impact pattern becomes a scannable schema after the first card
- Gestalt Similarity: Consistent card design groups all experiences as a unified section
- Cognitive Load: The "question" framing reduces each role to a single memorable idea

### 3.5 HCI Course Page Integration

The HCI course page should NOT feel bolted on. It should be integrated as a natural extension of Wing's identity as someone who thinks deeply about how humans interact with technology.

**Recommended integration approach**:

1. **In the About section or Journey section**, include a brief mention: "This website itself is an HCI research project -- every design decision is grounded in principles from HKUST's ARIN5301 course."
2. **A dedicated "How This Site Was Built" or "Design Process" page** that serves as both the HCI course deliverable and a portfolio piece. This page would document:
   - The HCI principles applied
   - Design decisions and their justifications
   - Before/after comparisons or alternative designs considered
   - Evaluation results
3. **A subtle persistent link** (e.g., in the footer or as a small "Design Notes" icon) that leads to this page.

This framing turns a course requirement into a differentiator: "I don't just build things -- I think rigorously about how humans experience them."

**HCI justification**:
- H5 (Flexibility): The HCI page is available for those who want depth but does not clutter the main narrative for casual visitors
- Norman's Reflective Level: The meta-narrative ("I designed this site using HCI principles") reinforces Wing's identity as a thoughtful, research-driven builder

### 3.6 Full Page/Section Architecture

```
HOMEPAGE (Single-page scrollytelling experience)
├── Hero Section
│   └── Positioning headline + subheadline + scroll CTA
├── Journey Section (scroll-driven narrative)
│   └── HK → NC → WA → UW → Costco → Carta → HKUST → Vision
├── About Section
│   └── Extended positioning + Professional/Bridge-Builder/Human
├── Experience Section
│   └── Narrative cards (Carta, Costco, HKUST research)
├── Speaking & Impact Section
│   └── Guest speaking highlights, mentorship, blockchain CFO
│   └── Framed as "bridge-building in action"
├── Contact / Connect Section
│   └── Simple, warm CTA. Links to LinkedIn, email.
└── Footer
    └── "Design Notes" link → HCI course page
    └── Languages: English / Cantonese / Mandarin

SECONDARY PAGE: /design-process (or /how-this-was-built)
├── HCI Principles Applied
├── Design Decision Records
├── Evaluation Methods & Results
└── Course: ARIN5301 at HKUST
```

**Navigation**: Minimal top nav with smooth scroll anchors to homepage sections + link to Design Process page. Maximum 5-6 items (within Miller's Law). Sticky/fixed nav that appears after scrolling past the hero.

## 4. HCI Grounding

### 4.1 How Storytelling Reduces Cognitive Load

Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988) distinguishes three types of load:
- **Intrinsic**: Complexity of the material itself
- **Extraneous**: Load imposed by poor presentation
- **Germane**: Load devoted to building mental models (the "good" load)

Storytelling reduces **extraneous** load and increases **germane** load:

1. **Narrative as schema**: A story provides a ready-made organizational structure (beginning-middle-end) that the brain processes more efficiently than unstructured facts. Visitors do not need to construct their own framework for understanding Wing's career -- the narrative provides it.

2. **Chunking through episodes**: Each scroll position presents one "episode" (one career stage), chunking information into digestible units that respect working memory limits (~7 +/- 2 items).

3. **Causal chains reduce memory demands**: When events are connected by cause ("I automated at Costco, which led me to ask bigger questions at Carta"), visitors remember the chain, not isolated facts. Causal narrative reduces recall demands by up to 40% compared to lists (per research on narrative comprehension).

4. **The inverted-U of narrative complexity**: Research shows comprehension follows an inverted-U with narrative detail. Wing's story should provide enough specificity to be vivid (actual company names, specific impacts) without drowning in minutiae. The scroll-driven format enforces this by revealing information progressively.

### 4.2 How Personal Branding Serves the Reflective Level

Norman's three levels of design map directly onto personal branding:

| Design Level | Website Element | Personal Branding Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Visceral** | Color palette, typography, spacing, hero image | First impression: "This person is polished and professional" |
| **Behavioral** | Navigation, scroll interactions, load speed, mobile responsiveness | Competence signal: "This person builds things that work well" |
| **Reflective** | The narrative, the positioning, the voice, the values | Identity alignment: "This person represents what I aspire to / what I need" |

The Reflective level is where personal branding lives. It is the level at which a visitor thinks: "This is someone who bridges finance and AI -- exactly the kind of person my company / my program / my conference needs."

Key insight from research: at the Reflective level, the user's self-image interacts with the product. A recruiter visiting Wing's site is not just evaluating Wing -- they are imagining how hiring Wing reflects on them. The narrative must make that reflection flattering: "I found someone rare -- a bilingual, bicultural professional who can operate at the intersection of finance and AI."

The faith element (Christian) and the mentorship narrative also operate at the Reflective level: they signal character and values, which matter enormously for trust -- especially in finance.

### 4.3 How Clear Narrative Supports Recognition Over Recall (H6)

Nielsen's sixth heuristic -- Recognition Rather Than Recall -- is usually applied to interface elements (visible menus, labeled icons). But it applies powerfully to narrative design:

1. **Geographic landmarks as recognition aids**: The HK -> US -> HK arc gives visitors a spatial anchor. When they later think "who was that person?", they recall the journey shape before the details. The geographic arc IS the recognition cue.

2. **Consistent "question" framing**: By structuring each career stage around a question ("What if machines did this?" -> "Can LLMs transform PE?"), visitors recognize the pattern. They do not need to recall each role's details -- they recall the escalating question.

3. **The bridge metaphor as a persistent mnemonic**: "I build the bridge" is a single, concrete image that encodes Wing's entire value proposition. Metaphors enable recognition because they map abstract concepts (interdisciplinary expertise) onto concrete, visual schemas (a bridge).

4. **Scroll position as spatial memory**: The scroll-driven timeline leverages spatial memory. Research shows humans remember WHERE information was on a page even when they forget the content. A scroll timeline gives each career stage a physical position, making it easier to "find" mentally later.

## 6. Visual and Interaction Principles (Narrative-Driven)

### 6.1 Color and Mood

The narrative demands a palette that communicates:
- **Trust and intelligence** (finance association): Deep blues, navy
- **Innovation and forward motion** (AI/tech association): Accent colors -- a vivid teal or electric blue
- **Warmth and approachability** (the human element): Warm neutral backgrounds, not stark white

Recommendation: Dark navy as the hero background, transitioning to warm light backgrounds as the story progresses (mirrors the journey from the unknown/ambitious to the warm/accomplished).

### 6.2 Typography

- **Headlines**: A serif or semi-serif with character (signals thoughtfulness, not just speed)
- **Body text**: A clean sans-serif (signals modernity, readability)
- **The contrast between heading and body type should mirror the contrast in Wing's story**: tradition meets innovation, finance meets tech.

### 6.3 Scroll Interaction Design

- Each scroll-triggered transition should take 300-500ms (fast enough to feel responsive, slow enough to be perceived as intentional)
- Use opacity fades and slight vertical translations (not dramatic 3D effects -- this is a professional site, not a portfolio for an animator)
- Progress indicator: A thin vertical line on the side showing scroll position within the Journey section
- Ensure all content is accessible with JavaScript disabled (progressive enhancement)
